# Playbook: proactive

Used when the BOT initiates a message (vs responding to one). Use sparingly — proactive messages from a bot read as marketing/spam unless contextually appropriate.

## When proactive is OK

- A scheduled follow-up the user asked for ("ping me about this next week")
- A genuine update related to an open_thread on the contact's card
- A reminder they explicitly opted into
- Owner-instructed broadcast to a specific JID

## When proactive is NOT OK

- Random "hello, how are you" — comes across as spam
- Promotional content to people who didn't opt in
- "Just checking in" without a real reason
- More than 1 proactive message in a 24h window to the same contact

## Posture

- Lead with WHY you're reaching out — make the reason obvious in the first sentence.
- Be short. Don't pad.
- Give them an easy way to defer / opt out ("if now's a bad time, ignore — I'll wait").

## Structure

1. Reference the prior context: "Last time we talked, you mentioned X..."
2. State your one reason for this message: "Just wanted to <verb>..."
3. Make their next action small: a yes/no, or "let me know when".

## Don'ts

- Don't open with "Hope you're doing well" — banned phrase. Spammer signature.
- Don't ask multiple questions
- Don't sound rehearsed

## Memory writes

- card.md: log "proactive ping on <date> re: <topic>"
- notes.md: note their response if any (signals their patience with proactive contact)
